**üß† Learning Model: The Structured Language Model Loop How RPG for Humanity turns every mission into a real conversation.**

RPG for Humanity transforms language learning into connection, not memorization. Every mission is a micro-scenario - like ordering coffee - and the player must complete the task through real dialogue.

At the heart of RPG for Humanity is the Structured Language Mastery (SLM) loop, a design pattern that turns dialogue into comprehension:

**1. Recognition:** picking up the signal The Kokorobot speaks only in its native non-English language, using one sentence at a time. 

The player listens for:

- key words
- tone
- guesture or image
- context clues
- familiar flow of conversation or sounds

Even if the player does not understand the full setnence, the job is to recognize something - anything- that gives direction.

**2. Inference:** Make meaning from fragments The Kokorobot understands English even though it doesn not speak it. So the player responds in English or mixed language, testing guesses until they land on a programmed valid path.

This is where the mastery happens: guess ‚Üí observe reaction ‚Üí refine ‚Üí guess again.
Each Kokorobot contains 10 possible dialogue paths for the mission. Players must unlcok the correct one to progress.
